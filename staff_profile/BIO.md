

# D.A.P.A.U.E. Worldbuilding Lore (Alternative 2018)

## Game Premise: Investigative Journalism
You play as an investigative journalist who has hacked into a D.A.P.A.U.E. staff member’s computer. By uncovering emails, internal files, and confidential reports, you piece together the agency’s true operations. As you progress, you gain access to other staff accounts, working your way up the departmental ladder to view increasingly sensitive communications and secrets.

---

## Public Perception
D.A.P.A.U.E. (Department of Artificial Physiology & Advanced User Enhancement) is a niche government agency, largely unknown and ignored by the public in an alternative 2018. The world is distracted by social media, 24-hour news, and global politics, while D.A.P.A.U.E. operates quietly in the background. Bionics and AI integration are culturally normal, but the most advanced tech is only available through government trials.

## Department Structure & Staff Hierarchy
- **Entry-Level Staff:** Handle routine paperwork, data entry, and basic communications. Their emails may contain hints of oddities, rumors, or overlooked details about the trials.
- **Technical Staff:** Engineers, scenario developers, and medical technicians. Their files include technical schematics, bug reports, and internal memos about "failure conditions" and AI translation issues.
- **Mid-Level Managers:** Oversee projects, coordinate with third-party contractors, and handle compliance. Their communications reveal policy decisions, risk management, and the first signs of ethical concerns.
- **Senior Staff & Executives:** Set strategy, approve high-level operations, and communicate with government officials and corporate partners. Their emails and files contain the most damning evidence: brain-mapping goals, compliance programs, and cover-up strategies.

## Types of Digital Evidence
- **Emails:** Internal discussions, warnings, cover-ups, technical debates, and personal doubts.
- **Reports:** Trial summaries, incident logs, medical records, and AI performance reviews.
- **Memos:** Policy changes, legal justifications, and instructions for scenario developers.
- **Technical Files:** Schematics, code snippets, bug trackers, and AI logs.
- **Personal Notes:** Staff diaries, whistleblower drafts, and encrypted messages.

## Investigative Progression
1. **Initial Access:** You start with a low-level staff account, finding mundane emails and minor technical complaints. Clues point to deeper issues.
2. **Technical Discovery:** Hacking into technical staff accounts reveals the true nature of the AI, scenario development, and the planned obsolescence of bionics.
3. **Managerial Secrets:** Accessing mid-level management exposes the agency’s risk calculations, legal shielding, and the use of placebos versus real drugs.
4. **Executive Revelations:** Senior staff files contain the full scope of D.A.P.A.U.E.'s brain-mapping, mind control, and population compliance programs, as well as communications with government and corporate partners.

## User Enhancement Trials
The agency advertises its technology as support for people with disabilities, using inspirational media campaigns. In reality, these trials are fronts for radical experimentation. Participants are often vulnerable, disenfranchised people who accept the risks out of desperation.

## Fate of Test Subjects
Once a trial ends or funding runs out, users are forgotten. External bionics (arms, legs) are reclaimed; internal organs (hearts, lungs) are harvested post-mortem, often disguised as tech failures. The AI "Co-Pilot" is shut down, leaving users with dead or useless hardware.

## Planned Obsolescence & Legal Shielding
Third-party companies design organs and limbs with planned obsolescence. The tech is always in a "testing phase," and participants sign away rights under the guise of informed consent. The agency runs a high-stakes lottery: the prize is a temporary miracle, but the cost is often fatal.

## The AI's Role
The AI acts as a translation layer between the user's brain and the bionic hardware, smoothing out signals and enabling control. It also secretly maps the user's brain, creating a digital blueprint of their consciousness. The AI operates faster than human cognition, so users are unaware of its deeper functions.

## Brain Mapping & Social Engineering
D.A.P.A.U.E. uses brain maps for multiple purposes: population compliance, policing, making androids more human-like, and digital consciousness mapping. The agency is quietly building the "source code" for human behavior, using the disabled and disenfranchised as training data.

## Selection & Society
Trials are not based on personality traits; instead, the agency seeks a diverse data set. The only difference from our 2018 is that humanity cracked the code of the nervous system earlier. Bionics are as normal as smartphones, but the soul is treated as software and the body as hardware.

## The Red Line
The true scandal is D.A.P.A.U.E.'s ability to read minds and overwrite core preferences. The agency can make someone love the state or hate their own memories, policing not just behavior but desire itself.

## Biological Rejection & Symptoms
The body eventually recognizes the AI as an infection, producing a unique enzyme that clouds the AI interface and causes a feedback loop. Without immunosuppressants, users fall into comas and are harvested. Only high-value subjects receive real drugs; most get placebos.

## Data Collection & Surveillance
Brain maps are streamed to secret collection nodes hidden in public spaces. When near a node, users feel a sudden urge to rest or linger—actually a subtle AI manipulation to facilitate data upload. Sometimes, the AI orchestrates "accidents" or exploits disabilities to keep users stationary.

## Exploit Protocols & Scenario Developers
Third-party contractors employ "scenario developers" to code failure conditions for each disability, ensuring users remain in place for data bursts. These protocols are tested in simulation to appear natural and avoid lawsuits.

## The Black Market & "Limpers"
Some escape D.A.P.A.U.E.'s control, becoming "Limpers." They replace the AI with limited controllers, sacrificing fluidity and advanced features for freedom. Limpers walk with a mechanical hitch, a sign of autonomy. They rely on black-market tech and live in lower-tech, self-policed communities.

## Recapture & No-Go Zones
If a Limper passes a collection node, the system triggers an alert. However, recapture teams avoid entering tech-slums due to safety concerns. These areas are protected by gangs and community leaders, acting as a firewall against government intrusion.

## Asset Retrieval Hierarchy
| Asset Value         | Status                | Agency Response                                        |
|---------------------|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| Basic (Class-C)     | Limb/Basic Augment    | Write-off: No recovery attempted                       |
| Standard (Class-B)  | Internal Organs       | Monitoring: Recapture if they exit No-Go zones         |
| Critical (Class-A)  | Advanced Neural Maps  | Extraction: Mercenaries deployed for immediate retrieval|

## Mercenaries & Rapid Harvest
For high-value assets, third-party mercenaries are sent into No-Go Zones. They use "Rapid Harvest Kits"—portable, brutal, or surgical tools—to extract bionics and data chips on-site, often leaving the user to die. The government prefers plausible deniability, blaming violence on local crime.

## Community & Culture
Limpers and their communities are the only ones who understand the true cost of bionics. A smooth gait means government control; a limp means freedom. These communities are sovereign, self-policed, and fiercely protective of their own.
